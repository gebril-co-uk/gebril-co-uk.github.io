    Gebril El-Fallah
    Lecturer in Mechanical and Materials Engineering

    Dr Gebril El-Fallah was appointed as Lecturer in the Mechanics of Materials Group within the University of Leicester's School of Engineering in early 2022. Before arriving in Leicester, Gebril completed a bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering (2013) and a master's degree in Nuclear Engineering from Imperial College London (2015) followed by a PhD at University of Cambridge (2019), where he worked in the Phase Transformations and Complex Properties research group. He was a Research Associate in Cambridge for one year following his PhD, a Teaching Fellow and a Director of Studies at Wolfson College, Cambridge. Gebril also holds a Fellowship of the Higher Education Academy (FHEA).

    Yuqi Xie
    PhD Student — 2025
    Phone: +44 000000000

    WeChat ID: QiiiiToki7

    Xie Yuqi graduated in 2025 from the Leicester International Institute at Dalian University of Technology with a degree in Mechanical Engineering. In the same year, she began her PhD studies at the University of Leicester, funded by the China Scholarship Council (CSC). Her research, titled "Sustainable Alloys for Industrial Waste Management Applications," addresses the growing need for advanced materials in environmental remediation, chemical processing, and wastewater treatment. The project aims to develop high-resilience alloys capable of enduring extreme corrosive and mechanical stresses, conditions under which conventional materials like stainless steel often degrade. Aligned with global sustainability goals, the research integrates computational modelling, advanced metallurgy, and experimental validation to accelerate the design of next-generation alloys.
