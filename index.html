    Welcome to Advanced Phase Transformations Research Group, where we delve into the captivating world of material science and alloy design. Our mission is to unravel the intricate movement of atoms in solids, driven by parameters as diverse as composition, stress, and temperature. At the heart of our endeavours lies the pursuit of transformation — a profound shift in the arrangement of atoms that unlocks endless possibilities. Our specialized group is at the forefront of alloy design, pioneering novel materials and processes that push the boundaries of what's possible. We're not just scientists; we're architects of matter, crafting alloys with precision and purpose. What sets us apart is our holistic approach, spanning Chemistry Design, Process Design, and advanced characterization techniques. We don't just create alloys; we engineer them from the ground up, tailoring their properties to meet specific needs. Our expertise in microstructural and mechanical characterization ensures that every alloy we create is not just innovative but also reliable. Join us on this thrilling journey through the world of materials, where we seek to transform atoms into remarkable alloys, shaping the future of technology and innovation. Explore our website to discover the limitless potential of material science, and welcome to a new era of possibilities.

    About

    Profile

    Dr. Gebril El-Fallah was appointed as Lecturer in the Mechanics of Materials Group within the University of Leicester's School of Engineering in early 2022. Before arriving in Leicester, Gebril completed a bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering (2013) and a master's degree in Nuclear Engineering from Imperial College London (2015) followed by a PhD at University of Cambridge (2019), where he worked in the Phase Transformations and Complex Properties research group. He was a Research Associate in Cambridge for one year following his PhD, a Teaching Fellow and a Director of Studies at Wolfson College, Cambridge. Gebril also holds a Fellowship of the Higher Education Academy (FHEA).

    Research

    Gebril's research interests span a broad range of fields in materials and metals. His main expertise is in bainitic steel where he designs new alloys for energy efficient automotive and aerospace applications and looks at the mechanism of structural evolution leading to ultimate failure, in what is the world's first bulk nanostructured steel.
